Permit Cheat Sheet

Timed Entry

A timed-entry reservation ($2 on recreation.gov) is separate from your park entrance pass ($35/vehicle for 7 days, or your America the Beautiful pass). Bear Lake Road is enforced 5 a.m.–6 p.m.; the rest of the park is enforced 9 a.m.–2 p.m.

June 1 Booking Playbook

Do Not Miss

All of July releases at once at 8:00 a.m. MDT on June 1. Wednesday's Bear Lake+ permit is the competitive one — book it first.

  1. Before June 1 (this week)

    Create a recreation.gov account, download the app, save a payment method. Decide which name/vehicle the reservations go under.

  2. Know exactly what you're buying

    Two "Timed Entry + Bear Lake Road" reservations: Tue 7/28 (afternoon, ~3–5 PM) and Wed 7/29 (earliest morning window). One reservation per vehicle per day.

  3. June 1, ~7:55 a.m. MDT

    Be logged in on the RMNP Timed Entry page, tab open and ready.

  4. 8:00 a.m. sharp — book Wednesday first

    Rocky Mountain NP → Timed Entry + Bear Lake Road → Wed July 29 → earliest window. Check out fast ($2 fee).

  5. Then book Tuesday

    Repeat for Tue July 28, a ~3–5 PM window (buffer after your flight + check-in).

  6. Optional — Thursday/Friday

    If you can't start the west-side hikes before 9 AM, also grab a regular "Timed Entry" (no Bear Lake) for those dates.

  7. Buy your entrance pass

    Separate from timed entry — $35/vehicle 7-day pass online, or bring your America the Beautiful pass.

  8. If you miss a window

    A second batch drops at 7:00 p.m. MDT the night before each arrival date. Set a backup alarm.

  9. Save everything offline

    Screenshot all confirmations — cell service is spotty at the gates.

Know Before You Go

Altitude

Sea level to 8,000–12,000 ft, fast. Hydrate hard, take day one easy (that's why Sprague is first), and watch the kids on Trail Ridge.

Afternoon storms

July thunderstorms roll in most afternoons. Be off high, exposed terrain by early afternoon — another reason for early starts.

Parking

Bear Lake lots fill before 8–9 AM. Wednesday's early permit handles it; the free Bear Lake shuttle is a good backup.

Wildlife

Best moose odds: dawn near water (Sprague, Coyote Valley, Kawuneeche Valley). Keep two bus-lengths away.

Pack

Layers + rain shell, sun protection, closed-toe shoes (required for the zipline), refillable water, snacks, quick-dry clothes for rafting.

Two passes, not one

Timed-entry reservation ≠ entrance pass. You need both on Tue–Thu.
