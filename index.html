At the Park Gates

Permits Booked ✓

All four timed-entry reservations are confirmed under recreation.gov order 0868381225 (see the Reservations table above). Here's what matters now.

  1. What to show

    At the entrance station, have your reservation ready in the recreation.gov app (or a screenshot) plus a photo ID. One reservation covers the whole vehicle.

  2. Buy your entrance pass (around July 1)

    Timed entry is not your entrance fee. The $35/vehicle 7-day pass wasn't on sale yet — a reminder is set to grab it around July 1 on recreation.gov (or bring your America the Beautiful pass).

  3. Time your arrival

    Enter within your booked window. Wednesday's 6–8 AM Bear Lake+ start also beats the parking crunch (Bear Lake lots fill by 8–9 AM). Thursday and Friday's 9–11 AM windows give flexibility, but earlier is still better for wildlife and afternoon storms.

  4. Save everything offline

    Screenshot all four passes before you go — cell service is spotty at the gates.

Know Before You Go

Altitude

Sea level to 8,000–12,000 ft, fast. Hydrate hard, take day one easy (that's why Sprague is first), and watch the kids on Trail Ridge.

Afternoon storms

July thunderstorms roll in most afternoons. Be off high, exposed terrain by early afternoon — another reason for early starts.

Parking

Bear Lake lots fill before 8–9 AM. Wednesday's early permit handles it; the free Bear Lake shuttle is a good backup.

Wildlife

Best moose odds: dawn near water (Sprague, Coyote Valley, Kawuneeche Valley). Keep two bus-lengths away.

Pack

Layers + rain shell, sun protection, closed-toe shoes (required for the zipline), refillable water, snacks, quick-dry clothes for rafting.

Two passes, not one

Timed-entry reservation ≠ entrance pass. You need both on Tue–Thu.
